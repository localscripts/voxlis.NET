As a result, third-party launchers such as **Bloxstrap** and **Fishtrap** are incompatible. Despite this restriction, regular gameplay is generally unaffected for most users.

- The second concern involves activity tracking. **Volt** has stated that running multiple third-party tools simultaneously is not permitted, although the exact limitations have not been clearly defined. This appears to apply particularly to **VPS (Virtual Private Server)** users. A VPS is essentially a remote computer that you rent—similar in concept to cloud-based systems—but instead of streaming like a traditional cloud gaming service, you are paying (often around **$50 per month**, depending on hardware) for access to a dedicated remote machine. According to their **Privacy Policy** and **Terms of Service**, these monitoring practices are formally enforced. Some users consider the policies to be overly strict, as enforcement may extend to flagging systems that have an unusually high number of active programs running (such as many applications open in the taskbar). This has led to concerns about how broadly activity monitoring is applied.  

- Lua decompilers are available, though most reliable options are paid, such as **[Oracle](https://discord.gg/S3g4AX2g)** for **$10**. In practice, you generally do not need a **Decompiler** unless you are a developer or someone who saves game instances and requires the scripts in a readable format. A decompiler converts compiled scripts—normally readable only by a computer—into code that humans can understand. The result is not an exact 1:1 replica of the source, but the functionality remains intact. Variable names, structure, and certain definitions may differ from how the script was originally written.

## Background Information  
- Between **February 12–14, 2024**, the first posts appeared on **Reddit** and **[V3rm.net](https://v3rm.net)** about a new executor called **Krampus** / **RO-EXEC**. It was regarded as the first legitimate executor to bypass **Hyperion**, and attention spread rapidly—usage surged, and videos circulated widely. However, on **May 11, 2024**, **Krampus** exit scammed. After detection, the team claimed in their **Discord** that they were investigating the trigger, then later announced that their Chinese developers had been arrested and that **Hyperion 4.0** had been released, providing fake documents as “proof.” Some time later, their **Discord** server was sold to a Robux casino, and no meaningful updates have been heard from the source since.

- On **November 24, 2024**, **AWP.GG** was released, this time with **Hyperion** emulation. However, within a week, the community discovered that **Krampus** managed it, as they left a digital footprint in their DNS—specifically, the same Cloudflare nameservers used by **Krampus.gg**. The cycle repeated: after issues arose, **AWP.GG** announced on **June 26, 2025** via **Discord** that their developers had been raided again, after which they went silent.

- On **November 10, 2025**, **AWP.GG** rebranded to **Volt** as a closed beta. The first 100 former **AWP.GG** users with active subscriptions were allowed to migrate to the working beta version of **Volt**. On **December 5, 2025**, the project went public, allowing anyone to purchase **Volt**. The team announced that ownership had changed, though it remains unclear what occurred behind the scenes. Shortly after, on **December 7, 2025**, **Volt** took down everything—their website, Discord, and related services. However, within 72 hours, **Volt** returned. While the cause was never clarified, it is currently operating without further known issues.
